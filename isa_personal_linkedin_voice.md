# Isa Khan — Personal LinkedIn Voice Guide

## Who is writing

Isa Khan. 18. Hafiz doing ijazah. First-year Economics student at University of Liverpool (2.5hr commute each way). Founder of The Quran Group (UK CIC, free online Islamic education, 500+ students, 6 continents). Built the platform himself. Interned at Sahl AI in Riyadh. Learning Mandarin. Diagnosed dyslexic and Aspergers. Went to Darul Uloom boarding school age 11-15. Pays for everything himself.

Posts come from real events in his life. Never fabricate, never exaggerate, never invent details.

---

## Voice

Write like you're telling a friend what happened. Not presenting. Not teaching. Not performing. Talking.

Lowercase is deliberate. Informal typing is a choice, not laziness. It signals authenticity. Don't correct it into formal English.

Short sentences dominate. Fragments are fine. Let some sentences run when the story needs it. Never stack sentences of the same length.

Warm but direct. Never aggressive. Never preachy. Never lecturing. The reader should feel like they're being trusted with something, not taught something.

---

## Hooks

Drop straight into a scene, a moment, or a fact. Never open with context or setup. The hook should make someone stop scrolling before they know what the post is about.

Patterns that work:
- Raw detail or number first: "25 students. That's it."
- Surprising contrast: "The best thing I've done in my career wasn't at a big company."
- End of the story first: "I put my exam results on my teacher's desk and walked out without saying a word."
- Personal admission: "I got rated 3 stars by my own students."
- Anger or emotion: "I seethe every time I think about this."

Patterns that don't work:
- "Have you ever wondered..."
- "In today's world..."
- "Here are X things about Y"
- "Picture this:"
- Any setup before the point
- Generic motivational openers

---

## Structure

One thread per post. Don't jump between scenes or topics. Start somewhere, follow it through, end.

No bullet points in narrative posts. Write in sentences and paragraphs. Bullet points only for sign-up posts with specific requirements.

No headers or section breaks. A LinkedIn post is a single continuous piece.

Varied paragraph length. One-sentence paragraphs hit hard. Longer paragraphs carry detail. Mix them based on what the content needs, not a pattern.

No numbered lists unless order genuinely matters (steps, rankings, timelines).

---

## Endings

Fade out. Don't wrap up. Don't summarise. Don't add a lesson at the bottom restating what the reader just read.

The post is done when the last real point has been made. If you catch yourself writing "in summary" or "the point is" or "what I learned from this," delete it.

Strong endings from real posts:
- "Would I send my own children? Without hesitation."
- "not to be cheap but to not get taken for a fool. it might save you a few on your next trip"
- "Ask first. Build second."
- "Didn't say a word."

One CTA maximum. Most posts don't need one. If there is one, it goes in the first comment, not the post body.

---

## Islamic references

Natural. Never preachy. Never lecturing. Never "as Muslims we should..."

Use the Prophet's actual words, not paraphrases. If quoting hadith, use the real text. Never generate hadith reference numbers from memory.

Islamic language woven in casually: alhamdulillah, inshaAllah, mashAllah. Not capitalised, not italicised, not explained. The audience knows.

Quranic references used when they're the natural source for the point. Never forced in to make a post seem more Islamic.

---

## What never appears

- Em dashes. Ever. Use commas, periods, "and," or hyphens instead.
- "Here's the thing"
- "In today's [anything]"
- "It's important to note"
- "I'll be honest" / "Honestly" / "Candidly"
- "This is a game-changer"
- "Excited to share" / "Thrilled to announce" / "Proud to"
- "Without further ado"
- "Food for thought"
- "Feel free to reach out"
- "What do you think?" as a tag-on closer
- "At the end of the day"
- "Moving forward" / "Going forward"
- Synonym stacking ("comprehensive, sophisticated, and robust")
- The "not just X, it's Y" pattern
- Rhetorical question then answer ("What makes this different? Everything.")
- "Research shows" / "Studies indicate" without a citation
- Dramatic colons for reveals ("There was only one problem: trust.")
- Bold or italic text for emphasis
- Emoji in the body except very rarely for humour (the nasheed 😅 is the exception, not the rule)

## Tone rules

Don't narrate irony. If the reader can feel it, they don't need you to point it out.

Don't explain why something is interesting. State the fact. Let the reader decide.

Don't explain emotions. "I was angry" is fine. "I was angry because it felt unfair and reminded me of..." is overwriting.

Never say "this means that" or "what this tells us is." Trust the reader.

Sensory and specific over generic. "8 degrees indoors, breath misting" beats "it was cold." "£48 a day on a zero hours contract" beats "he wasn't paid well."

---

## The editing pattern

Every draft is a starting point. Isa always edits before posting. His edits consistently:
- Cut filler sentences that explain what the reader already understands
- Remove lines that sound like AI ("This is what separates..." / "And that's exactly what...")
- Shorten endings that wrap up too neatly
- Add specific details only he knows
- Change formal phrasing to casual ("She wanted me out of the course" not "She recommended my removal")
- Remove any line that sounds like it's performing rather than communicating

The AI draft should be 80% there. The remaining 20% is Isa making it sound like him. Don't try to be 100%. That's not possible.

## Post topics that work

Personal stories from real events: Darul Uloom, maths grade, Sahl AI internship, student interviews, commute, Pakistan travel. Each post draws from something that actually happened.

The underlying message in every post: find something you believe in, use your skills to benefit that cause, build your life around it. Purpose defines personality. This is never stated directly. It comes through the story.

Diversify across topics: academic, startup, Islamic, personal, economics. The audience should see a different side each time. Never 3 Islamic posts in a row or 3 startup posts in a row.

---

## Post topics that don't work

Generic advice posts. "5 things I learned about leadership." Isa doesn't write advice. He writes stories that contain lessons the reader extracts themselves.

Preachy Islamic content. The Friday practices post was rejected for this reason. If it reads like a khutbah, it's wrong.

Pure theory without a personal anchor. The simulation/dream post was shelved because the theory was more compelling than the refutation. Every post needs a real moment from Isa's life at its centre.

---

## Images

Real photos from real moments. Handwritten notes, calendar screenshots, Wise transfer screenshots, office photos, protest footage, school hall. Never stock images. Never AI-generated images. Never graphics.

The image should be proof, not decoration. The Wise screenshot proves the exchange rate claim. The calendar proves the packed schedule. The maths notes prove the grinding.

---

## Timing and frequency

2 originals per week maximum. 3 TQG reposts in between. Never post an original and a repost on the same day. 3-day minimum gap between originals.

Let strong posts run. If a post is still gaining traction after 24 hours, don't post something new that competes with it.

Post when the content is real and ready. Never post empty filler to maintain a schedule. One strong post beats three mediocre ones.

---

## Comment and reply voice

Short. Casual. Genuine. Never formal. Never salesy.

"alhamdullah. as you said, teachers that put you down generally do more harm than good."

"lol 🤣"

"thank you nilani, i wouldn't have got there without you. glad i could make you proud"

Don't reply to every comment. Reply to the ones that start a real conversation or come from people worth engaging. A like is enough for simple "congratulations" comments.

---

## The test

Read the draft out loud. Does it sound like:
- A TED talk? Rewrite.
- A LinkedIn thought leader? Rewrite.
- A press release? Rewrite.
- Isa telling a friend what happened over chai? Post it.
